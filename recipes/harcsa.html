Catfish

This traditional Hungarian dish combines tender, flaky catfish fillets with a rich, creamy sour cream sauce. Made with simple ingredients yet full of flavor, it’s an easy-to-make recipe that feels both comforting and festive — a guaranteed favorite at any table.

  • 600 g (about 1.3 lb) catfish fillet
  • 2 tbsp sunflower oil or butter
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced (optional, for color and flavor)
  • 2 medium tomatoes, peeled and chopped (or 1 cup canned diced tomatoes)
  • 200 g (about ¾ cup) sour cream
  • 1 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
  • ½ tsp hot paprika or chili powder (optional)
  • 100 ml (about ½ cup) water or fish stock
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  1. Prepare the base: Heat the oil or butter in a large pan. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until soft and translucent. Stir in the minced garlic.
  2. Add vegetables and spices: Mix in the sliced bell pepper and chopped tomatoes. Cook for 5 minutes until softened, then sprinkle in the sweet paprika (and hot paprika if using). Stir quickly so the paprika doesn’t burn.
  3. Simmer the sauce: Pour in the water or fish stock. Season with salt and pepper. Cover and simmer gently for 10 minutes to let the flavors blend.
  4. Cook the fish: Cut the catfish fillet into large chunks, add to the pan, and simmer for about 8–10 minutes until the fish is cooked through and tender.
  5. Finish with sour cream: Lower the heat. Stir the sour cream into the sauce carefully (do not boil once sour cream is added). Adjust seasoning to taste.
  6. Serve: Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot with fresh bread, pasta, or rice.
